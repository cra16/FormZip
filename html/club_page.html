가족같은 분위기, 전산 동아리 CRA

The background-image property, when applied to an image, refers to (drum roll ... ) the background-image of the image. It will always be behind the image. If you want the image to appear in front of the image, you are going to have to use two images, or another container with a background-image that covers the first image. BTW, it is bad practice to rely on !important for overriding. It can also be ineffective since 1) it can't override declarations in an element's style attribute, and 2) it only works if it can work based on the markup and the current CSS. In your case, all the huffing and puffing and !important declarations won't make an image do something it can't do.
